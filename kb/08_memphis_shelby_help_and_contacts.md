# Memphis / Shelby County help and contacts

This section lists places in Memphis and Shelby County that may help with conservatorship, legal problems, and protecting your family.

---

## Shelby County Probate Court (where conservatorships are filed)

Shelby County Probate Court  
140 Adams Avenue, Room 124  
Memphis, TN 38103  

Clerk’s Office hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 am – 4:30 pm.  
Phone: (901) 222‑3750  
Fax: (901) 222‑3756  

What they do:

- Handle conservatorships and guardianships.  
- Handle wills and estates.  
- Handle name changes, some mental health hospitalizations, and birth certificate corrections.

The clerk’s office can:

- Explain basic court process and forms.  
- Tell you filing hours and where to file.

The clerk’s office **cannot**:

- Give you legal advice.  
- Tell you what to put in your petition.

Email for copies: probate.copies@shelbycountytn.gov (fees apply).  
General help email: probatehelp@shelbycountytn.gov

## Adult Protective Services (APS) – Tennessee

If an older adult or adult with a disability is being abused, neglected, or financially exploited:

- Call Tennessee Adult Protective Services (APS): 1‑888‑277‑8366.  
- You can also report online at reportadultabuse.dhs.tn.gov

APS can:

- Investigate abuse, neglect, or exploitation.  
- Try to reduce the risk and connect people with services.

## Legal help in Memphis / West Tennessee

These groups may offer free or low‑cost civil legal help depending on income and case type:

### Memphis Area Legal Services (MALS)

- Phone: (901) 523‑8822  
- Website: http://www.malsi.org

They can often help with:

- Elder law and conservatorship questions.  
- Housing, benefits, and consumer issues.

### West Tennessee Legal Services (WTLS)

- Phone: (731) 423‑0616  
- Website: https://www.wtls.org

Helps people in West Tennessee counties outside Shelby with civil legal issues.

### Other legal aid in Tennessee

If you have family in other parts of Tennessee:

- Legal Aid of East Tennessee – https://www.laet.org  
- Legal Aid Society of Middle Tennessee & the Cumberlands – https://las.org 

State‑wide help:

- Tennessee Alliance for Legal Services (TALS) – runs a legal help line and referral network.

## Consumer and scam help

If you suspect fraud, scams, or bad business practices:

- Tennessee Attorney General – Division of Consumer Affairs  
  - Phone (Nashville): (615) 741‑4737  
  - Toll‑free in TN: (800) 342‑8385  
  - Online complaint: via the Attorney General’s consumer complaint page.

- Better Business Bureau (BBB) of the Mid‑South (Memphis area)  
  - Phone: 901‑759‑1300 or 800‑222‑8754  
  - Website: https://www.bbb.org/us/tn

These offices can take complaints about scams, unfair businesses, or contractors who take money and do not do the work.

## Aging and disability services

Area Agency on Aging & Disability:

- Statewide number: 1‑866‑836‑6678 – connects you to your local Area Agency.

They can help with:

- Finding local services for older adults and adults with disabilities.  
- Information on benefits, long‑term care, and caregiver support.

## When to reach out

Reach out to these contacts if:

- You are not sure where to file or which forms you need (Probate Court clerk).  
- You see signs of abuse, neglect, or exploitation (APS).  
- You need legal advice about conservatorship, hospital problems, or protecting money and property (legal aid or private attorney).  
- You think someone is scamming or overcharging your loved one (Attorney General, BBB).

Keep a note of:

- Who you called.  
- Date and time.  
- What they said.

This can help you and your lawyer later.
